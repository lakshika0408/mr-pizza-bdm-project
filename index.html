Business Context

Three Core Problems

Mr. Pizza is a single-operator B2C food outlet in Kathgodam, Uttarakhand run by Mr. Prakash Singh Danu. Data collected from handwritten sales notebooks covering 5 months of operation.

📉
Problem 01
No Demand Forecasting
Revenue dropped 26.1% from September to December with no system to anticipate it — causing over-procurement in October and stockouts in late December.
  • No weekly procurement target
  • Seasonal decline caught owner off-guard
  • Flat average useless given CV of 78%
📦
Problem 02
No Inventory System
47 menu items managed by guesswork. 12 items drive 69.2% of revenue — yet procurement treats all items equally, misallocating budget and storage.
  • No ABC priority classification
  • Class C items over-stocked
  • Class A items at stockout risk
📊
Problem 03
No Pattern Insight
Tuesday generates only 36% of Sunday's revenue — a structural gap the owner had no visibility on. No mechanism to stimulate demand on slow days.
  • Sunday = 24.1% of all weekly revenue
  • Tuesday structural underperformance
  • No customer retention mechanism

Methodology

Analytical Framework

Three complementary methods applied to 997 transaction records, chosen for their implementability by a one-person operation with no software beyond a mobile phone and notebook.

01
Time Series Analysis
4-Week Moving Average (MA4) Forecasting
MA4(t) = [R(t) + R(t−1) + R(t−2) + R(t−3)] ÷ 4. Applied to 23 weeks of data to smooth out day-to-day noise (CV = 78%) and produce a rolling weekly procurement target. Chosen over ARIMA and Exponential Smoothing because only 5 months of data are available — ARIMA typically requires 2+ years. The MA4 is also directly computable by the owner in a notebook without software.
02
ABC Inventory Classification
Cumulative Revenue Contribution Ranking
All 47 items ranked by revenue share. Cumulative Share(k) = Σ Share(i) for i=1 to k. Class A (≤70%): 12 items → always in stock with 2-day buffer. Class B (≤90%): 9 items → weekly review. Class C (>90%): 26 items → order on demand only. EOQ was considered but rejected — requires complete COGS data which is unavailable given incomplete purchase bill records.
Class A · 12 items · 69.2% revenue Class B · 9 items · 20.3% revenue Class C · 26 items · 10.5% revenue
03
Day-of-Week Pattern Analysis
Demand Pattern Identification
Day_Share(d) = R(d) ÷ Σ R(all days) × 100%. SUMIF applied to Day column across 139 trading days. Weekend days generate 79% more revenue per day than weekdays (₹31,885 vs ₹17,841). Sunday = 24.1% of weekly revenue; Tuesday = 8.6%. Cohort analysis was considered but not feasible — the notebook records no customer identification data, making true retention metrics impossible to compute.
Recommendations

SMART Action Plan

Six recommendations across three problem statements — all designed for a one-person operation requiring no technology beyond a notebook and mobile phone.

P1 · Immediate · Week 1
R1 — MA4 Procurement Rule
Every Friday evening: add the past 4 weeks' revenue and divide by 4. This is the procurement target for next week. Under 5 minutes. No software needed.
  • Target: procurement error <10% of actual demand
  • Expected: save ₹3,000–5,000/month in winter wastage
P1 · Medium Term · Nov 2024
R5 — Seasonal Adjustments
Apply empirically derived seasonal indices: Oct −4.5%, Nov +2.7%, Dec −7.3%, Jan −10.4%. Note: January requires caution, not uplift.
  • Target: procurement variance within 5% by Dec 2024
  • Expected: protect ₹34,000+ January revenue
P2 · Immediate · Week 2
R2 — Class A Stocking Protocol
Maintain 2-day buffer of 7 shared ingredients: cheese, pizza base, tomato sauce, capsicum, corn, onion, paneer. Friday checklist — 5 minutes.
  • Target: zero Class A stockouts within 4 weeks
  • Expected: protect 69.2% of revenue (₹1,05,854 per 5 months)
P2 · Immediate
R3 — Stop Pre-Stocking Class C
Cease purchasing ingredients for 26 Class C items unless a customer places an order. Frees procurement budget and reduces perishable wastage immediately.
  • Target: 30% reduction in procurement complexity
  • Expected: immediate cash flow relief
P3 · Within 1 Month
R6 — Tuesday Combo Offer
Kulhad Pizza + Cold Drink at ₹109 (~10% off combined price of ₹119). Promote via handwritten sign at entrance. Zero additional cost.
  • Target: 20% increase in Tuesday revenue in 8 weeks
  • Expected: +₹520/month from Tuesday uplift alone
P3 · 1–3 Months
R7 — WhatsApp Contact Collection
Collect customer numbers at point of sale for Tuesday offer notifications via WhatsApp. Aim for 100+ contacts in 3 months. No app needed.
  • Target: 100+ contact list within 3 months
  • Expected: direct retention communication channel
